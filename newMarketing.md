# Marketing Plan — Acadiana Web Design

**Owner:** Layken
**Written:** 2026-04-17
**Review cadence:** Weekly check-in, monthly channel review, 90-day full reset

---

## Honest Starting Point

The existing outbound pipeline (see `marketing.md`) is built and working mechanically: searches run, leads are scraped and scored, audit emails send, demo pages render. That machine is good.

The problem is not the pipeline. The problem is the bottleneck immediately after it — the human conversion step. Cold dialing is the hardest possible channel, and it is not a fit for the founder's current working mode. A month of paralysis is the evidence.

This plan accepts that reality and routes around it. Cold calls are demoted from "primary channel" to "optional follow-up tool, used only on warm leads." Everything else in the plan is picked for one reason: it matches the founder's actual advantages (local, veteran, face-to-face capable, one person accountable) instead of fighting them.

Two things that feel like problems but are not:

- **"Most scraped leads already have decent websites."** That means the AI scoring is working. The qualified pool is the minority — that is by design. Don't force bad-fit prospects. Run more searches to grow the qualified pool, or change channels entirely to find prospects already raising their hand.
- **"A GoDaddy $40/mo user won't pay $199/mo."** Correct for most of them. They are not the target. The target is: businesses with no site, an unmaintained site, or a site so bad it's costing them calls — owners who already feel the pain, not owners who need to be convinced pain exists.

## Channel Framework — Ranked for This Business

### Tier 1 — Run these now. Every week.

These are the channels that match the founder's personality and unfair advantages.

| Channel | Why not |

## 90-Day Execution Plan

### Days 1-30 — Prove human conversion works

**Goal:** Close the first 2-3 cold-started clients through the lowest-friction channels. Build the muscle. Collect real objections.

**Targets:**
- 2 referral calls to existing clients — Week 1
- 20 in-person drop-ins with printed audits — across the month
- 5 partnership conversations (Chamber, accountants, sign shops, insurance agents) — across the month
- **Outcome target:** 2-3 new signed clients, 3-5 warm follow-ups in progress

**Not doing in this phase:** Paid ads. New tools. New channels. Don't spread thin.

**Why this phase matters:** Until a cold prospect signs, every strategy conversation is theoretical. The first 3 closes teach more than the next 10 books on marketing.

### Days 31-60 — Add one paid channel, measure

**Goal:** Compare paid reach against organic/in-person to find cost per closed client by channel.

**Targets:**
- Continue Tier 1 weekly cadence (drop-ins, referral asks, partnership touches)
- Launch Google Search Ads with ~$300 test budget on 10 high-intent local keywords
- Launch one programmatic SEO roundup page ("Best Plumbers in Lafayette" or similar)
- **Outcome target:** 3-5 more clients closed; first signal on paid CAC

### Days 61-90 — Compound what's working

**Goal:** Double down on winners, cut losers, layer retargeting on top.

**Targets:**
- Identify the highest-ROI channel from the first 60 days and increase spend/time there
- Add Facebook retargeting to audit-page traffic
- Build the second/third programmatic SEO page
- **Outcome target:** Total 8-12 clients closed, repeatable pipeline from at least 2 channels

### Day 90 — Full review

Measure cost per closed client by channel. Decide: which channels stay, which get cut, which deserve more budget. Only now is it fair to evaluate whether an outside tool (AI marketing agent, VA, contractor) earns its keep.

## Tier 1 Playbooks

### Playbook A — Client Referrals

**Who:** All About Towing (Alexandria, LA) and TB Tree Service (Central LA). Add every future client to this list the month after they go live.

**Offer them:** $50 Visa gift card per referral that becomes a paying client. (Gets mailed after the referred client's second monthly payment clears — avoids gaming.)

**The ask — phone call, not text:**
> "Hey [name], Layken. Quick favor — I'm looking to help 2-3 more local business owners this month with their websites. You know anyone in trades who either doesn't have a site or has one that's driving them crazy? If you can text me their name and number, I'll reach out and mention you sent me. And if any of them sign up, I'll send you a $50 Visa card as a thank-you."

**Weekly:** One touch per active client. Rotate. Don't ask the same client twice in 30 days.

**Track in admin:** Which client referred which prospect. Tag the prospect so the gift card triggers automatically on their second payment.

### Playbook B — In-Person Drop-Ins

**Who to target:** Highest-scoring qualified leads (fitScore 8+) already in the admin from scraped batches. Sort by geographic cluster — batch drop-ins by area to minimize driving.

**Before leaving:**
1. Print the audit report for each prospect (PDF the `/audit/{token}` page)
2. Staple a business card to each
3. Handwritten post-it: *"Layken — no pitch, just dropped this by. Call if you want to talk."*

**Script at the door:**
> "Hey, is [owner] around? I'm Layken, local web developer — veteran-owned, based in Acadiana. I ran a free audit on your website and wanted to drop it off in person. No pitch. Take a look when you get a chance, call me if anything stands out. Appreciate your time."

**Then leave.** Do not pitch unless asked. The goal is to plant a physical, memorable impression. Most of the close will happen on the follow-up call 3-5 days later — which is now a warm call because they have your audit in their hand.

**Cadence:** 5-10 drop-ins per run, 2 runs per week. Batch by city.

### Playbook C — Local Partnerships

**Who to approach:**

| Partner type | Why they have your customer |
|---|---|
| Chamber of Commerce (Lafayette, Acadia, Lafayette Economic Development Authority) | Official directory of local SMBs |
| Accountants / bookkeepers serving small business | They talk to 50+ owners monthly about their business finances |
| Sign and vehicle-wrap shops | Same customer, adjacent spend |
| Insurance agents (commercial lines, trades) | Same customer, trusted advisor role |
| Business-facing print shops | Business cards → website upgrade is a natural pairing |

**Offer:** 10% of Year 1 MRR per referred client (= ~$240/client over 12 months). Or a flat $200 on close. Their pick.

**Script — email or in-person:**
> "Hey [name], I'm Layken, local web developer — I do a $199/mo fully-managed website plan for service businesses. Veteran-owned, based here in Acadiana. I pay 10% of the first year for any client you refer — works out to about $240 per client over the first year. If that's something worth a quick coffee, happy to buy. No pressure."

**Chamber specifically:** Join the local Chamber ($200-400/yr). Go to one mixer per month. Don't pitch — meet people. Be the "web guy" in the room. One good Chamber relationship per quarter pays for the whole year of dues.

## Tier 2 Playbooks (Activate Days 31-60)

### Playbook D — Google Search Ads

**Targeting:**
- Geo: Acadiana parishes only (Lafayette, St. Martin, Vermilion, Acadia, Iberia, St. Landry)
- Schedule: Business hours only

**Keyword list (exact match, tight):**
- `web designer lafayette`
- `website designer acadiana`
- `website for plumber louisiana`
- `website for contractor louisiana`
- `fix slow website`
- `small business website louisiana`
- `cheap website design louisiana`
- `affordable web designer`
- `website maintenance louisiana`
- `google ads website not working` (intent: they tried and it didn't work)

**Landing page:** Drive to a dedicated landing page, not the homepage. Single offer: "Free Website Audit — see exactly what's broken and what it's costing you." Email-gated. On submit, trigger the existing audit flow or a manual version.

**Budget:** $300-500/mo. Kill any keyword with zero conversions after 30 clicks. Double down on the ones that convert.

**Kill criteria:** If 60 days in, CAC is above $400 per closed client, pause and rework. If it's below $200, scale budget.

### Playbook E — Facebook/Instagram Retargeting

**Audience:** Anyone who visited an `/audit/{token}` page but did not book a consultation (use Meta Pixel + custom audience).

**Creative:** 3 variations, rotate:
1. Screenshot of audit report + "Your audit is waiting. Book a 15-min call."
2. Testimonial card — All About Towing quote + photo
3. "$0 down, $199/mo, live in 72 hours. Still thinking it over?"

**Budget:** $3-5/day. This is awareness + recency, not primary acquisition.

### Playbook F — Direct Mail

**Who:** Same qualified-lead list as drop-ins, but for prospects geographically too far to visit in person.

**What:** Print the audit report (color, bound or stapled), handwritten cover letter on letterhead, business card. Padded mailer envelope so it's opened.

**Cover letter template:**
> "[Owner name] — I'm a local web developer here in Acadiana. I ran a free audit on your website and printed it out for you. Pages X-Y are the parts costing you calls. No pitch — if you want to talk, my number's on the card. Otherwise, keep it as a reference. — Layken, Acadiana Web Design, veteran-owned"

**Cost:** ~$3 per piece all-in. 20/month = $60. Track with a unique URL or phone extension per mail campaign.

### Playbook G — Programmatic Local SEO Pages

**First page:** `/guides/best-plumbers-lafayette-la` (or similar). Publish a roundup of the top 10-15 plumbers in Lafayette ranked by reviews, with a short blurb each. Link to their websites (or note "no website" as a data point).

**Second touch:** Email each business featured:
> "Hey [name], just wanted to let you know you're featured in my roundup of the best plumbers in Lafayette — link below. No catch, I'm just trying to rank this page on Google. If you ever want to upgrade your site, I'm local. Either way, appreciate what you do."

This reverses the dynamic. You did them a favor. They open your email. Conversion on this approach is dramatically higher than cold outbound.

**Scale:** One page per month to start. Plumbers Lafayette → Roofers Lafayette → Tree Services Acadiana → and so on. The scraper already has the data; this is a layout + publish task.

## On Outsourced Marketing Tools (e.g., AI Marketing Agents)

External services like `agent.enrichlabs.ai/marketing` and similar AI marketing agents are skipped in this plan for now. Not because they're bad in principle, but because they're the wrong tool at this stage, for these reasons:

1. **The pipeline in `marketing.md` already does what most AI marketing agents do.** Scrape, score, audit page, personalized email, follow-up — that's the same loop. The bottleneck isn't top-of-funnel automation, it's the human conversion step after contact.
2. **The moat in this business is local + physical + veteran.** No AI agent can walk into a plumber's shop in Lafayette. Outsourcing the outreach commoditizes the one thing that isn't commoditized.
3. **Learning must come before delegation.** With zero cold-started closes so far, there's no internal instinct yet for what moves a prospect in this specific market. Handing that learning to a vendor means never developing the judgment — and when the vendor underperforms (common for niche local offers), there's nothing to fall back on.
4. **Unit economics probably don't pencil.** Most of these services run $200-1,000/mo. At $199/mo MRR per client, 1-5 attributable closes per month are needed just to break even. Unlikely in a local trades market.

**Revisit condition:** After 10-20 clients are closed and there's a clear sense of what converts, *and* top-of-funnel volume is the actual bottleneck (not bottom-of-funnel conversion), then evaluate outsourced tools against a known baseline.

## Metrics to Track

Keep it small. Track these five numbers in a simple weekly spreadsheet or in the admin. Nothing else matters until these move.

| Metric | What it tells you | Target (Month 1) | Target (Month 3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Qualified leads generated / week** | Top of funnel health | 20 | 40 |
| **Contacts made / week** (drop-ins + referral asks + partnerships touched) | Activity — is the plan being executed | 10 | 20 |
| **Conversations had / week** (actually talked to a decision-maker) | Real engagement | 3 | 8 |
| **Signed clients / month** | Only one that pays the bills | 2-3 | 4-6 |
| **Cost per closed client, by channel** | Where to spend next | N/A | Under $400 |

**Weekly review (15 minutes, same day/time every week):**
- Did I hit my activity targets?
- What objections came up? Any pattern?
- Which channel produced the most conversations?
- What am I changing next week?

**Monthly review (60 minutes):**
- Cost per closed client by channel
- Kill any channel that's above $400 CAC after enough data (30+ clicks, 10+ touches, etc.)
- Double budget on any channel below $200 CAC

## Decision Rules

**When to add a new channel:**
- Current channels are hitting targets consistently (3+ weeks in a row)
- There's visible capacity (not at saturation on existing channels)
- There's a clear hypothesis about *why* the new channel will work

**When to kill a channel:**
- 60 days of consistent effort with zero closes
- Cost per closed client is above $400 after sufficient data
- It's consuming time that would produce more revenue elsewhere

**When to revisit cold calling:**
- After Tier 1 is running cleanly and at least 5 clients have closed through other channels
- Only on warm leads (prospects who viewed their audit, replied to an email, were referred, etc.)
- Never as primary outbound

**When to consider an outsourced marketing service:**
- 10+ closed clients through own channels
- Clear top-of-funnel bottleneck (not conversion bottleneck)
- Can articulate what "good" looks like so the vendor can be held accountable

## Mindset Anchors (from `marketingHelp.md`, worth repeating)

- **Activity beats perfection.** A mediocre drop-in that actually happens beats a perfect call script that never does.
