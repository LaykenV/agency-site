5Scripts library

Memorize the flow, not the words. Sound like a neighbor, not a salesman.

Cohort B warm call (phone-only lead, no site)

"Hey, is this [Business Name]? — Hi [Name if you got it], my name's Layken, I run Acadiana Web Design here in Youngsville. I was doing some research on local [tree services / landscapers / towing] in the area and came across y'all — looks like y'all've got solid reviews but I noticed you don't have a website yet, is that right? [Let them answer.]"These are warm because: you researched them, you know their service, you confirmed the gap. Don't pitch in the opener — make them tell you they have the problem. If they push back ("we're fine without one"), don't argue — thank them and move on.
Branch responses
"Yeah we don't have one, just Facebook" → "Yeah, that's actually super common around here. I do $199/month websites — no upfront cost, live in 72 hours, I handle everything. Worth a 10-minute call this week to see if it makes sense?" "We've been meaning to" → "Yeah I hear that a lot. The reason I called — I do this for local trades for $199/month, no money down. Would you be open to me texting you a 2-minute video of what I built for another Louisiana service business?" "Not interested" → "No problem, appreciate you taking the call. Have a good one." [Hang up. Don't push.]"Not selling, filtering." If they're not a fit, you just saved 20 minutes. The right ones self-identify.
