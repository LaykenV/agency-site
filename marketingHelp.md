# Cold Calling Cheat Sheet

Quick-reference guide for cold calls. Keep this open while dialing.

---

## Before You Call

1. Pull up their Google listing (reviews, rating, phone, address)
2. Check their current website (or lack of one) on your phone — note load time
3. If you ran the pipeline, glance at their fit score, pain points, and outreach angle
4. Have their audit link ready if one exists

---

## Openers

Pick the one that matches their situation. Keep it under 15 seconds.

### They Have No Website

> "Hey, is this [Name / the owner]? My name's Layken, I'm a local web developer here in Louisiana. I came across your business on Google — you've got some great reviews. I noticed you don't have a website set up yet and I had a quick idea for you. Do you have 60 seconds?"

### They Have a Slow / Ugly / Builder Site

> "Hey, is this [Name / the owner]? I'm Layken, local web developer. I was looking at businesses in [city] and pulled up your site — you've got great reviews but your website is loading pretty slow on mobile. I actually ran a speed test on it. Got a minute?"

### They Have a Decent Site But You Spotted Issues

> "Hey [Name], I'm Layken, I do websites for local service businesses. I came across your site while looking at [industry] companies in [city] — looks good overall, but I noticed a couple things that might be costing you calls. Got a quick second?"

### They Were Referred or You Have Mutual Context

> "Hey [Name], I'm Layken — I built the website for [referral's business name]. They mentioned you might be looking for something similar. Got a minute to chat?"

### Voicemail (Keep It Under 20 Seconds)

> "Hey [Name], it's Layken, local web developer. I saw your great reviews on Google and had a quick idea about your website. Give me a call back when you get a chance — [your number]. Thanks."

Don't leave more than one voicemail. Follow up with an email or audit link instead.

---

## The Conversation Flow

### 1. Hook (0-15 sec)
Use one of the openers above. End with a question to get them talking.

### 2. Diagnose (15-60 sec)
Ask about their current situation. Let them talk.

- "Do you have a website right now, or are people mostly finding you through Google Maps?"
- "Are you getting calls from your website, or is most of your business word of mouth?"
- "Who set up your current site? Are you happy with it?"
- "When someone Googles [their service] in [their city], do you show up?"

### 3. Agitate (30-60 sec)
Connect their situation to a real cost. Don't be pushy — just state facts.

- **No website:** "Right now when someone searches '[their service] in [city]', your competitors with websites are getting those calls instead of you. Even just one or two jobs a month from a website would more than pay for it."
- **Slow site:** "I ran your site through Google's speed test and it scored [X] out of 100. Google actually pushes slow sites down in search results, so you might be invisible to people searching on their phones — and over 60% of local searches are on mobile."
- **Builder site (Wix/Squarespace/GoDaddy):** "Those platforms are easy to set up, but they're heavy and slow. Google scores them pretty low for mobile speed, which means you're probably not showing up as high as you should in search results."
- **Outdated site:** "Your reviews are great — people clearly love your work. But when someone lands on your site and it looks like it hasn't been updated, there's a disconnect. They might call the next guy instead."

### 4. Present (30-60 sec)
Keep it simple. Three points max.

> "Here's what I do — I build fast, professional websites for service businesses like yours. Everything's included: the design, hosting, domain, unlimited changes, support. It's $199 a month, nothing upfront. I can have your site live within 72 hours of our kickoff call."

If you have a audit page ready:

> "I actually already ran a free audit on your website — it shows your speed score, the issues I found, and what a modern site would look like. I can text or email you the link right now if you want to take a look."

### 5. Close
Don't overthink this. You're just asking if they want to move forward.

> "Want me to send you a link to get started? It takes about 2 minutes to sign up, and then we hop on a quick kickoff call to nail down the details."

Or softer if they need time:

> "Tell you what — let me send you that audit report and my info. Take a look, and I'll follow up in a couple days. Sound good?"

## They Say / You Say

### "I already have a website."
> "Yeah, I took a look at it actually. Your Google PageSpeed score is [X] — that's how Google rates your site for mobile users. Anything under 70 and Google starts burying you in search results. I can get you to 95+ without spending a dime upfront."

### "How much does it cost?"
> "$199 a month, nothing upfront. That covers the design, hosting, domain, unlimited changes — everything. There's a 12-month commitment, then it's month-to-month after that."

### "$199/month is a lot."
> "I get it. But think about it this way — what's one new job worth to you? $500? $1,000? One new customer from your website pays for 3 to 5 months of my service. The question isn't whether $199 is a lot — it's how many jobs are you losing right now without a good website."

### "I don't want a contract / 12 months is too long."
> "Totally fair. The 12 months is just so I can invest the time to build you a custom site without charging you upfront. After that it's month-to-month — you can cancel anytime. I have to earn your business every month."

### "I need to think about it."
> "No pressure at all. Let me send you a free audit report I put together for your website so you can see what I'm talking about. Can I follow up with you next week?"

(Then send the audit link and set a follow-up in your admin panel.)

### "Can you just build it for a flat fee?"
> "I could, but then you'd be on your own for hosting, updates, security, and any changes you need down the road. My clients love that they never have to think about their website. If something needs changing, they just tell me and it's done — no extra charge."

### "I already tried a website and it didn't work."
> "That's actually really common. Most of the time it's because the site was slow, wasn't built for mobile, or wasn't set up for local search. That's the stuff I specialize in — fast sites that actually rank on Google."

### "I'm not interested."
> "No worries at all. If you ever need anything website-related down the road, I'm local — feel free to reach out. Have a good one."

(Mark them as "not interested" in your admin and move on. Don't argue.)

### "How is this different from Wix / Squarespace / GoDaddy?"
> "Those are DIY builders — you're doing all the work yourself, and the sites tend to be slow and generic. I hand-build everything, the site loads in under a second, I handle all the updates, and you get a dedicated web person you can reach out to anytime. No tech headaches."

### "Can I see examples of your work?"
> "Yeah, absolutely. I'll send you a link to a site I built — loads in under a second. I also ran a free audit on your current site if you want to see what I'd fix."

(Send them your live client sites + their audit link if available.)

### "I know a guy who does websites."
> "Nice — if you're happy with them, stick with them. But if you ever want a second opinion or they're not getting you results, give me a call. I'm local and I'm not going anywhere."

### "My nephew / cousin / friend built my site."
> "That's great that you had someone to help. The main thing I'd check is your mobile speed — pull up your site on your phone and see how fast it loads. If it's sluggish, that's costing you calls. I can run a free speed test for you if you want."

### "Send me some info / an email."
> "For sure. What's the best email? I'll send you a free audit report I put together for your site — shows your speed score and the issues I found."

(This is usually a polite brush-off. Send the audit link, then follow up by phone in 3-5 days. The audit report is what converts these.)

## Closing Phrases

When the conversation is going well and it's time to move forward:

- "Want me to send you the signup link? Takes about 2 minutes."
- "I can have an audit report ready for you by [tomorrow/end of day]. Want to hop on a quick 15-minute call to go over it?"
- "If you're good with everything, I'll shoot you a link to get started and we can do a kickoff call this week."
- "Sound like something you'd want to try? There's no upfront cost, so there's really no risk."

## After the Call

### They Said Yes
1. Create prospect in admin (or convert from scraped lead)
2. Send welcome email with magic link
3. They sign agreement, start subscription, submit build details
4. Schedule kickoff call
5. Build and deliver

### They Said "Send Me Info"
1. Send audit link via text or email
2. Set 3-5 day follow-up in admin
3. Follow up by phone — "Hey, did you get a chance to check out that audit report I sent?"

### They Said "Let Me Think About It"
1. Send audit link
2. Set 5-7 day follow-up
3. Follow up once. If still no, set a 30-day reminder and move on.

### They Didn't Answer
1. Leave one voicemail (see script above)
2. Send audit link via email if you have it
3. Try calling back at a different time of day (early morning or late afternoon works best for trades)
4. After 3 attempts with no answer, move on

### They Said No
1. Mark "not interested" in admin
2. Move on immediately. Don't dwell.

## Mindset Reminders

- **You're not selling.** You're finding people with a problem and offering a solution. If they don't have the problem, they're not your customer.
- **Rejection is just filtering.** Every "no" gets you closer to the right "yes." Most people won't need what you're offering — that's normal.
- **You already have proof.** You have live clients, real testimonials, and a real product. You're not asking anyone to take a leap of faith.
- **They're busy, not rude.** If someone is short with you, it's because they're in the middle of a job. Don't take it personally.
- **One job pays for months.** A plumber gets one $800 job from your site and your service is paid for 4 months. The math is on your side.
- **Activity beats perfection.** A mediocre call you actually make beats a perfect script you never use. Just dial.
- **The first 10 calls are the hardest.** After that, it becomes routine. Push through the discomfort.

## Daily Routine

1. **Check follow-ups** (15 min) — Open admin, call anyone due for follow-up today
2. **Run pipeline** (15 min) — Search a new city+industry if needed, review qualified leads
3. **Call block** (60-90 min) — Aim for 8-12 calls per session. Best times: 8-10am or 4-6pm (before/after jobs)
4. **Send audit links** (15 min) — Email audit links to anyone you couldn't reach or who asked for info
5. **Update admin** (10 min) — Log notes, set follow-ups, mark statuses
